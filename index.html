About this model

This is a deliberately modest model. The persuasion function δ(s) = α(1 − e−βs) caps the share of the electorate any spender can shift, and shows the diminishing returns that political scientists consistently find. Disclosure multiplies that yield by (1 − λ), modeling the counter-speech and press scrutiny that arrive when spending is visible in real time.

The point of the model is not to predict elections. It is to demonstrate something more modest and harder to argue with: that under conservative parameters, concentrated spending can flip a plurality outcome at a small fraction of what a single 2024 donor actually spent—and that real-time disclosure changes the picture entirely.

A more rigorous version, with formal derivation of the flip threshold and engagement with the doctrinal landscape since Citizens United, appears in a forthcoming article. The argument for a non-academic audience lives at Limited Factors.
